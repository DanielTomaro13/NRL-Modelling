Six stat models + three scoring models

The explorer above covers the six continuous stat markets (tackles, run metres, hit-ups, post-contact metres, performance points, runs). Scoring is handled by three more models — a try-scorer (Poisson on tries), a goal-kicking model (Poisson on goals), and player points built by convolving them (points = 4·tries + 2·goals + field goals). Their out-of-sample calibration is on the backtest, their projections on Scoring, and every market lines up against the books on Compare odds.

The method, in four steps

1. Predict the mean. A separate gradient-boosting model per stat, trained on Champion Data feeds with strictly pre-match features (rolling form shifted one game, opponent defence conceded, team context). Counts use Poisson loss.

2. Build a distribution. The prediction is the mean; σ is calibrated from out-of-sample residuals as σ = α + β·mean (spread grows with volume). That gives a full Normal(mean, σ), not just a point.

3. Price the line. Probability of going over = the normal tail above the line, with a ±0.5 push band on whole-number lines and a 50/50 split on quarter lines — standard book convention.

4. Find the edge. De-vig the book's two prices to a market probability, compare to the model, and compute EV per dollar (a push returns the stake). Positive EV = the price looks too long.
